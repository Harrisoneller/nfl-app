# Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-05-14_

This is a template. Replace bracketed sections with your own values before
public launch and have a lawyer review for your jurisdiction.

## What we collect

- **Account information** — if you sign up: email address and display name.
- **Usage data** — pages visited, features used, timestamps, AI requests and
  approximate token counts (used for cost management).
- **Technical data** — IP address, browser type, request IDs for debugging.

We **do not** collect: real names beyond what you provide, payment information,
location data, social-media profile data, or contact lists.

## How we use it

- To operate the service (authentication, request routing, rate limiting).
- To prevent abuse (rate limits keyed by IP or user ID).
- To manage AI cost ceilings (token totals per user).
- To debug issues (request IDs in error reports).

We do **not** use your data to train AI models. AI inputs are sent to our
chosen LLM provider (Grok by default, configurable). See that provider's
privacy policy for their data handling.

## Third-party data sources

The app aggregates content from third parties. We don't store more than
necessary to render and credit each item back to its source:

- ESPN (public endpoints), nfl-data-py (open data), Sleeper, RSS feeds, Reddit,
  Open-Meteo (weather). News headlines and links are stored; full article text
  is not.

## Retention

- AI chat history: retained until you delete the chat session or your account.
- Saved widgets: retained until you delete them.
- Access logs: 30 days.
- Error reports (Sentry): per Sentry's defaults.

## Your rights

- **Access** — request a copy of your data at any time.
- **Delete** — request account deletion; we will remove your user-keyed data
  within 30 days.
- **Opt-out** — sign out and stop using the service; account deletion is
  permanent.

Contact: [your email]

## Children

The service is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect
data from children under 13.

## Changes

We will update this page when we materially change how data is collected
or used. Continued use after such changes constitutes acceptance.
